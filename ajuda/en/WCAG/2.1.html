Checkpoint 2.1

Ensure that all information conveyed with color is also available without color, for example from context or markup.

Do not use colors to transmit information, unless the point of view of the notation/text is clear, because without the color the information become senseless.

Example 1

There are two ways to do things. The second method, that appears in red, is the prefered one.

  1. Blue procedure.
  2. Red procedure.

This example can easily be seen in devices that doesen't have anly color.

Example 2, wrong

There are two ways to do things. The prefered method is in red.

  1. Procedure 1.
  2. Procedure 2.

In this example, is incomprehensible to locate the information, because it will only be located if the user could see through a color monitor.
